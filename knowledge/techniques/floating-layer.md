# Floating Layer (Three-Pedestal Lift)

**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Best for:** Background panels, sky layers, depth illusions, image frames, portrait panels behind foreground action

## How It Works

Three identical rectangular pedestals (strips of cardstock folded in half lengthwise) are glued perpendicular to the base card. A flat panel is glued across their tops. The three-point support (two at back, one at front) creates a stable triangle — no wobble. When the card closes, the pedestals fold inward and the panel folds down.

## Geometric Constraints

- All 3 pedestals must be **exactly the same height** `h` — any variation causes tilt
- Pedestal width: ≥ 5mm each
- Position: two pedestals at rear edge of panel, one centered at front edge
- Floating panel must fit within card boundaries when closed
- Card height must be at least `h + panel thickness + 5mm` to close flat
- Pedestals fold inward when card closes — ensure they don't collide with other elements

## Step-by-Step

1. Cut three identical strips of 80 lb cardstock. Strip flat width = `h × 2`, strip length = pedestal footprint width
2. Score and fold each strip in half lengthwise — this creates the pedestal shape
3. Mark three glue positions on the base card (two at back, one at front-center of where the panel will sit)
4. Glue pedestals at the three marked positions, perpendicular to the base card surface
5. Apply glue to the tops of all three pedestals
6. Press the floating panel down onto all three simultaneously — alignment is critical
7. Allow to dry with card open; test close/open action before proceeding

## Template Markup

- Base card: mark three pedestal glue positions with X patterns
- Pedestal strip: show flat dimensions with score line down center
- Floating panel: mark glue tabs at three attachment points

## Common Uses in Scenes

- Sky or horizon background behind a foreground scene
- Photo frame floating above the base
- Secondary scene layer (e.g., window view behind a room)
- Text panel above a main mechanism
