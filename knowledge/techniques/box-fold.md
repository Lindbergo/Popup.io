# Box Fold (Parallel Fold Pop-Up)

**Difficulty:** Beginner–Intermediate
**Best for:** Tables, stages, platforms, buildings, shelves — any flat horizontal surface that pops up

## How It Works

Two equal-length cuts are made from the gutter edge of a folded card, parallel to each other. A new fold connects their inner ends, parallel to the gutter. This creates a rectangular flap that, when reverse-folded (against the direction of the main card fold), pops out as a box/platform perpendicular to the card surface when the card opens.

## Geometric Constraints

- Both cuts must be **equal length** — call this `d` (this becomes the box height)
- Distance between the two cuts = box width `w`
- Connecting fold runs parallel to gutter at distance `d` from the gutter
- Box top surface when open = `w × d`
- Minimum clearance from card edge: `d + 5mm`
- The connecting fold must be reverse-folded (opposite direction to the main card fold)

## Step-by-Step

1. Fold the card in half at the gutter
2. Mark two parallel lines on the folded edge, each of length `d`, separated by distance `w`
3. Cut along these two lines (cut through both layers of the folded card)
4. Mark and score the connecting fold at distance `d` from the gutter, parallel to gutter
5. Open the card. Reverse-fold the rectangular flap — push it toward you so it folds opposite to the main card fold
6. Crease the new fold firmly. Close the card — the box flattens. Open — it pops up
7. Add decorative legs, surface items, or additional mechanisms on top of the box

## Template Markup

- Cut lines: red solid bold lines on the folded edge
- Gutter fold: blue dashed
- Connecting fold: green dash-dot (it's a valley fold on the box face)
- Label `w` (box width) and `d` (box depth/height)

## Common Uses in Scenes

- **Table:** box fold = tabletop. Decorative strips glued to front face = table legs
- **Stage / platform:** box fold = stage floor. Curtains or figures glued on top
- **Building:** multiple box folds at different depths = cityscape layers
- **Shelf / counter:** single box fold with items glued to surface
