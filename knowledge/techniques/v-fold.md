# V-Fold

**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Best for:** Trees, flames, hearts, mountains, text letters, simple shapes that rise vertically

## How It Works

The base card is folded in half at the gutter. A separate piece is folded in half at its center (the V-fold crease). The two halves of that piece are glued symmetrically to the two halves of the base card with the piece's crease sitting on or parallel to the gutter. Opening the card forces the piece upright; closing folds it flat.

## Geometric Constraints

- The V-fold crease **must** sit on the gutter or on a line parallel to it
- Both halves of the piece must be mirror images for the element to stand straight
- Height of element when open = half the flat width of the piece
- The angle of glue attachment controls lean direction (left, right, or straight up)
- Glue lines on each half must be equidistant from the V-fold crease

## Step-by-Step

1. Cut the pop-up shape from 80 lb cardstock
2. Score and fold the piece in half at its center — this is the V-fold crease
3. Score and fold the base card in half at the gutter
4. Open the base card flat. Apply glue to the underside of the left half-tab of the piece
5. Align the piece crease with the gutter; press the left tab onto the left half of the base
6. Fold the card closed — the right half of the piece self-positions against the right base half. Press to bond
7. Let dry fully before testing

## Template Markup

- Draw the flat piece with its center fold line marked
- Mark glue tabs (hatched) on each half, equidistant from the V-fold crease
- Mark the gutter line on the base card showing where each half attaches

## Common Uses in Scenes

- Person standing → full-body silhouette, folded at waist
- Chair (side view) → L-shaped piece: seat + back
- Chair (front-on) → symmetric piece, seat visible from front
- Tree / plant → symmetric tree shape
- Any simple rising shape
