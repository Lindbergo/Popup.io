# Moving Arm (Pivot Mechanism)

**Difficulty:** Intermediate–Advanced
**Best for:** Waving hands, opening doors, nodding heads, clock hands, any element the reader controls by pulling a tab

## How It Works

A piece is anchored to the card at one fixed point (the pivot — a brad or glued pivot dot). A pull-strip runs through a slit in the card and connects to the other end of the arm. Sliding the strip moves the arm in an arc around the pivot. The user controls the position; there is no spring return.

## Geometric Constraints

- Pivot point to arm tip = arm length `L`
- Arc of motion is limited by card boundaries — the arm tip must stay within the card at all positions in the range
- Pull-strip width: 5–8mm
- Pull-strip length = arc of motion distance + handle length (minimum 20mm handle extending outside card)
- Slit for pull-strip: wider than strip by 1mm on each side
- Brad or pivot must be reinforced (cardstock washer on underside) to prevent tearing under repeated use

## Step-by-Step

1. Mark and punch the pivot point on the base card
2. Cut the slit for the pull-strip (parallel to intended direction of motion)
3. Thread the pull-strip through the slit from underneath
4. Attach the arm piece to the pull-strip at the correct end point
5. Fix the arm to the pivot point with a brad (preferred) or a small reinforced glue dot with a paper washer
6. Test the range of motion before gluing any decorative elements
7. Add decorative sleeve on underside to guide the pull-strip and prevent it from pulling free

## Template Markup

- Mark pivot point with a circle and cross (brad location)
- Mark slit with red solid lines
- Draw the arm in both extreme positions (dashed) to show range of motion
- Mark pull-strip dimensions and handle length

## Common Uses in Scenes

- Hand waving hello
- Door swinging open to reveal a room
- Clock hand pointing to a time
- Tail wagging on an animal
