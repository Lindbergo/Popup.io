# Rotating Disc

**Difficulty:** Advanced
**Best for:** Clock faces, wheels, spin-the-wheel interactives, kaleidoscope effects

## How It Works

A circular disc is mounted on a central brad at its exact center point. A window cut in the card front exposes the disc. A parallelogram keeper strip on the long side of the disc base keeps the disc upright as the card opens and closes. The reader can spin the disc freely.

## Geometric Constraints

- Disc diameter ≤ card height − 20mm (clearance all around)
- Brad must be at the **exact geometric center** of the disc — off-center brads cause wobble
- Window in card front must be ≥ disc diameter + 2mm on each side for clearance
- Keeper strip (parallelogram) width = arc length the disc base travels as card opens
- Brad reinforcement: cardstock washer on both sides to prevent tearing

## Step-by-Step

1. Cut the disc from 90 lb cardstock; mark and punch exact center
2. Cut the window in the card front panel (slightly larger than the disc)
3. Mark the exact center of the window on the base card — this is the brad position
4. Attach the disc to the base via the brad; add washers both sides
5. Close card gently to test that disc clears the card edges at all positions
6. Construct the parallelogram keeper strip and glue it to hold the disc base upright when card opens
7. Test full open/close cycle before adding any decoration to disc faces

## Template Markup

- Mark disc with center crosshair and brad hole
- Mark window on front panel (dashed outline — do not cut through card)
- Show keeper strip as a parallelogram with glue zones at both ends
- Label disc diameter and brad position

## Common Uses in Scenes

- Clock face with hour/minute hands drawn on the disc
- Spinning wheel (fortune wheel, color wheel)
- Planet with rotation effect
- Selector dial (e.g., "spin to choose your gift")
