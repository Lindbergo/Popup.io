# Photo Placement and Sizing

Rules for sizing, printing, and attaching photos to pop-up card elements.

---

## Core Rule

**A photo attached to a pop-up piece must never be larger than the piece it mounts on.**

Photo dimensions:
```
photo_width  = piece_width  − 4mm  (2mm border each side)
photo_height = piece_height − 4mm  (2mm border top and bottom)
```

---

## Minimum Readable Face Size

| Use | Minimum size | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Recognisable face (you know the person) | 30 × 40mm | Barely readable |
| Comfortable face recognition | 40 × 50mm | Recommended minimum |
| Clear face with expression visible | 50 × 65mm | Ideal for a gift card |
| Full upper body + face | 60 × 80mm | Best for seated figure cards |

**If the piece the photo mounts on cannot accommodate 30 × 40mm, do not attempt a photo placement.** Use an illustrated or printed figure instead, or increase the card size.

---

## Card Size vs. Photo Size

This is the most common design failure: the card is too small for readable photos.

### A2 standard (108mm half-width, 140mm tall)
With a box-fold table (d=30mm) and V-fold chairs (attached at p=40mm):
- Chair back available: **50 × 65mm** (from character-shapes.md)
- Photo fits: **46 × 61mm** ✓ — comfortable, face clearly readable

### Smaller cards (A6 folded: 78mm half-width)
- After box fold zone (d=20mm) and clearance (10mm): chair attached at p=30mm
- Available card depth for chair: 78 − 30 − 8 = **40mm max chair height**
- Chair back at 40mm height → photo max **30 × 36mm** — faces barely readable
- **Recommendation:** Do not attempt photo-on-chair designs on A6 cards. Use A2 minimum.

---

## Printing Instructions for the User

Always include these in the step guide when photo placements are present:

1. **Print at actual size** — do not let the printer scale the photo. Set print size to exactly the specified dimensions.
2. **Use photo paper or matte paper** — standard printer paper wrinkles when glued. 170gsm photo paper holds flat.
3. **Trim carefully** — use a craft knife and metal ruler, not scissors, for clean straight edges.
4. **Test fit before gluing** — hold the trimmed photo against the piece to confirm it fits before applying any glue.
5. **Use PVA glue sparingly** — a thin, even layer on the photo back only. Too much glue causes warping.
6. **Press flat for 60 seconds** — place a heavy book on top while the glue sets.
7. **Glue photos last** — always assemble and test the full mechanism before attaching photos. Photos are the final step.

---

## Multiple Photos (two people on chairs)

When placing photos of two different people on two chairs:

- Both photos must be the **same size** — different sizes look unbalanced and emphasise one person over the other
- Crop both photos to the same aspect ratio before printing
- Both photos attach at the **same step** in assembly
- If one person is significantly taller in the photo, crop tighter (head + shoulders only) to equalise

---

## When the Card is Too Small — Alternatives

If the design cannot accommodate a 30 × 40mm minimum photo:

| Alternative | When to use |
|---|---|
| Printed illustration (AI-generated or drawn) | Always works — not limited by size |
| Silhouette cut-out in the person's hair/clothing colour | Works well for figures on chairs |
| Initials or name only | Good for very small pieces |
| Larger card format | Increase to A5 or custom size if photos are essential |

---

## Worked Example: Birthday Table for Two (A2 Card)

Card: 216 × 140mm flat, 108mm half-width
Box fold table: d = 30mm
Chair attachment: p = 40mm (from clearance-rules.md)
Chair back: 50mm wide × 65mm tall (from character-shapes.md)

**Photo specs:**
- Print size: **46 × 61mm**
- Paper: matte photo paper, 170gsm
- Crop: head + shoulders, centred
- Attach at: final assembly step, after all mechanism testing is complete
- Glue zone: full back of photo, thin even PVA layer

**Printing check:** 46 × 61mm is comfortably above the 40 × 50mm recommended minimum. Faces will be clearly recognisable. ✓
