# Character Shapes for V-Folds

Which poses and silhouettes work as V-fold elements, and how to design them correctly.

---

## The V-Fold Shape Rule

A V-fold piece must:
1. Be **symmetric** about its vertical centre line — the left half is a mirror of the right
2. Fold flat without any part extending beyond the card edge when closed
3. Have a minimum stroke/limb width of **5mm** anywhere in the silhouette (thinner = tears)
4. Read clearly as a recognisable shape when reduced to a simple silhouette

---

## Poses That Work Well

### Standing person / figure
- Arms at sides or slightly raised (not spread wide)
- Fold line runs vertically down the centre of the body
- Each half: half-head, half-torso, one arm, one leg
- Works because body is naturally symmetric
- **Safe arm raise:** arms up to 45° from body — beyond that, arms create closed-footprint overhang

### Seated person (on a chair V-fold)
- Upper body only (torso + head), arms resting or slightly raised
- Mounted on top of the chair back panel
- Not a separate V-fold — glued flat onto the chair back piece
- Keep the torso width ≤ chair back width − 4mm

### Cat — sitting pose ✓ (recommended)
- Round head, pointed ears, compact body, front paws together or slightly apart
- Tail curled around the side — keep within the half-width of the piece
- **Ideal proportions:** head 40% of total height, body 60%, total height 60–90mm
- Sitting pose folds flat easily — no limbs extending far horizontally

### Cat — hugging pose ⚠ (use with caution)
- Arms spread wide create a large horizontal footprint when closed
- **Maximum safe arm span:** total width ≤ card_half_width × 1.4
  - Example: 108mm half-width → max total cat width = 151mm (75mm each side of fold)
- Arms must taper to ≥ 5mm width at the tip — wider is sturdier
- Paw tips must not exceed the card edge when folded flat
- **Test before finalising:** fold a paper prototype closed and check the arms lie flat

### Heart
- Perfect V-fold shape — symmetric, no thin strokes, compact
- Fold line runs vertically through the centre dip and bottom point
- Height = width for best visual proportion
- Works at any size from 30mm to 100mm

### Tree
- Symmetric triangular or layered shape — ideal V-fold
- Trunk can be a narrow rectangle below the canopy (minimum 8mm wide)
- Star or star shape at top: keep ≥ 6mm point width

### Animal — front-facing
- Bear, rabbit, owl: all work well — round and symmetric
- Ears pointing up stay within the piece outline — safe
- Wings spread (bird, butterfly): same caution as cat hugging pose — check arm span rule

### House / building
- Use box fold, not V-fold (see box-fold.md)
- A V-fold house works only for a gable-end view (triangle roof over rectangle walls)

---

## Poses That Do NOT Work

| Pose | Why |
|---|---|
| Person running (arms and legs spread) | Asymmetric, limbs extend in all directions — cannot fold flat |
| Animal in profile (side view) | Asymmetric — one half of the fold is blank |
| Wide bird in flight (wingspan > card width) | Wings extend beyond card edge when closed |
| Person waving with one arm raised | Asymmetric — one side heavier; piece leans when open |
| Group of 3+ figures side by side | Too wide; use floating layer or tunnel instead |

## Sizing Guide by Card Format

| Card half-width | Safe V-fold element max width | Max height (with 8mm edge clearance) |
|---|---|---|
| 108mm (A2 standard) | 130mm | 60mm at p=40mm attachment |
| 110mm | 132mm | 62mm at p=40mm |
| 148mm (A5) | 178mm | 100mm at p=40mm |

---

## Hugging Cat — Correct Dimensions for A2 Card

Based on clearance-rules.md (108mm half-width, attachment at 40mm from gutter):

- Total cat width (both arms spread): **max 150mm** (75mm each side of fold line)
- Cat height: **60mm** (folds within p=40mm + h=60mm = 100mm < 108mm edge)
- Arm width at tip: **minimum 8mm**
- Head diameter: **35–40mm**
- Body width at widest: **45mm**

If you want the arms wider, use a larger card (A5: 148mm half-width gives much more room).

## Chair Piece — Correct Construction

The chair is an L-shaped V-fold piece. Correct proportions for a photo-friendly chair back:

```
┌─────────────┐  ← chair back: 50mm wide × 65mm tall
│             │     (sized to hold a readable face photo)
│  [photo]    │
│             │
├─────────────┤  ← seat joint — score line here, fold to 90°
│   seat      │  seat: 50mm wide × 25mm deep
└─────────────┘
```

- Chair back width: **50mm** (photo-friendly minimum — see photo-placement-sizing.md)
- Chair back height: **65mm**
- Seat depth: **25mm**
- Flat piece total height: **90mm** (back 65mm + seat 25mm)
- Flat piece width: **50mm**
- The V-fold crease runs vertically down the centre of the assembled L-shape
- Each half of the piece is 25mm wide

**Attachment position:** 40mm from gutter (per clearance-rules.md for A2 card with 30mm box-fold table)
