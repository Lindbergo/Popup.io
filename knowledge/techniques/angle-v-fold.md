# Angle (Off-Center) V-Fold

**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Best for:** Elements that lean or pivot when the card opens — characters facing the viewer, directional shapes, angled architecture

## How It Works

Same construction as a standard V-fold, but the fold crease of the piece intersects the gutter at a non-right angle. The asymmetric attachment angle causes the element to lean in a specific direction when the card opens rather than standing straight up.

## Geometric Constraints

- The two glue lines on the base (one per half) must be mirror images across the gutter
- The tighter the angle to the gutter, the more the piece leans rather than stands upright
- Minimum angle from gutter: **15°** (below this, the piece lies nearly flat and the mechanism is weak)
- Maximum practical angle: **75°** (above this, behaves like a standard V-fold)
- Height of element when open varies with angle — test with a paper prototype before finalizing dimensions

## Step-by-Step

1. Cut and fold the pop-up shape as with a standard V-fold
2. On the base card, mark the glue line for the left half at your chosen angle to the gutter
3. Mirror that angle for the right half glue line (must be a true mirror across the gutter)
4. Glue left half first with card open flat, then close card to bond right half
5. Test: the element should lean consistently in one direction when card is fully open

## Template Markup

- Same as V-fold but glue lines are drawn at the chosen angle, not perpendicular to gutter
- Label the angle value on the template

## Common Uses in Scenes

- Person leaning forward as if reaching across a table
- Tree bending in wind
- A sign tilted toward the viewer
- Architectural element at a diagonal
