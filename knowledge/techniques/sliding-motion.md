# Sliding Motion (Pull-Tab)

**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Best for:** Characters entering or exiting from the side, sliding doors, text reveals, drawers opening

## How It Works

A slit is cut in the base card parallel to the direction of motion. A strip passes through the slit; the visible element is glued on top of the strip on the card face. A sleeve on the underside guides the strip and stops it pulling free. Pulling the exposed tab moves the element linearly.

## Geometric Constraints

- Slit length = range of motion + element width
- Strip length = slit length + handle (≥ 20mm) + sleeve engagement (≥ 15mm)
- Sleeve height must match strip thickness — just loose enough to slide freely without wobble
- Element must not extend beyond card edge at any position in the motion range
- Slit must be wider than strip by 1mm on each side

## Step-by-Step

1. Determine the element's start and end positions; calculate slit length from these
2. Cut the slit in the base card
3. Cut the pull-strip to the correct total length
4. Thread the strip through the slit
5. Glue the element to the top of the strip, centered over its starting position
6. On the underside, glue the sleeve strip parallel to the slit, spanning the full slit length
7. Slide the strip through the sleeve to ensure free movement — trim sleeve if too tight
8. Attach a decorative tab or handle to the pull end

## Template Markup

- Mark slit with red solid lines; label length
- Show strip flat dimensions; mark element glue zone (hatched)
- Show sleeve position on underside view
- Draw element in start and end positions (dashed) to confirm no edge overflow

## Common Uses in Scenes

- Character sliding in from the side of the card
- A drawer opening on a piece of furniture
- A sliding window or door panel
- A message revealed as a strip is pulled
